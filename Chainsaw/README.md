### 1. Safety First

**WARNING: Read all safety warnings and instructions before use.**

**Personal Safety**
* Always wear a helmet, eye protection, ear protection, and protective gloves.
* Always wear protective clothing (cut-resistant trousers) and non-slip safety boots.
* Keep all parts of the body away from the saw chain when the motor is operating.

![PPE Requirements](images/ppe.png)

**Work Area Safety**
* Keep children and bystanders at least 15m away from the work area.
* Do not use the chain saw in rain or wet locations.

### 2. Before You Operate

**Installing/Removing the Battery**
1. **To Install:** Slide the battery cartridge in until it clicks.
2. **To Remove:** Press the button on the battery while sliding it out.
    * ![Installing the battery](images/battery_install.png)

**Main Power and Speed Control**
1. **Turn On:** Tap the main power button. The mode lamps will light up green.
2. **Change Speed:** Tap the **Main Power Button** to cycle through High, Medium, and ECO (Low) modes.
3. **Turn Off:** Press and hold the main power button.
    * ![Power and speed controls](images/controls.png)

### 3. Operation Guide

**Starting the Saw**
1. Depress the lock-off lever.
2. While holding the lock-off lever, pull the switch trigger.
3. Release the switch trigger to stop.
    * ![Lock-off lever and trigger](images/trigger_operation.png)

**Using the Chain Brake**
* **To Activate:** Push the front hand guard forward. The chain should stop immediately.
* **To Release:** Pull the front hand guard back toward the handle.
    * ![Operating the chain brake](images/chain_brake.png)

**Checking Chain Lubrication**
* Check the oil tank level through the viewing window.
* **NEVER run the saw without Bar & Chain oil.**
* The tool is self-lubricating, but you can adjust the flow using the screw on the bottom.
    * ![Checking oil level](images/oil_check.png)

### 4. Maintenance

**WARNING: Always remove the battery before maintenance.**

**Adjusting Chain Tension**
1. Loosen the retaining nuts on the side cover.
2. Lift the tip of the guide bar.
3. Turn the adjustment screw clockwise to tighten the chain.
4. Tighten the retaining nuts once the chain fits snugly against the lower side of the bar.
    * ![Adjusting chain tension](images/tensioning.png)

**Cleaning and Care**
* **Cleaning:** Wipe the tool with a dry cloth and clear wood chips from the vents.
* **Bar Care:** Clean the guide bar groove and oil hole regularly to ensure lubrication.
* **Sharpening:** Keep the chain sharp to prevent motor strain and reduce kickback risk.
    * ![Cleaning the bar](images/cleaning_bar.png)

### 5. Troubleshooting

| Problem | Probable Cause | Remedy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Motor does not run.** | Battery empty or not installed. | Install a fully charged battery. |
| **Chain does not rotate.** | Chain brake is engaged. | Pull the front hand guard back to release. |
| **Saw not cutting well.** | Chain is dull or installed backward. | Sharpen the chain or check rotation direction. |
| **Chain is dry / Smoking.** | Oil tank empty or oil hole blocked. | Fill oil tank and clean the guide bar groove. |
| **Abnormal vibration.** | Loose guide bar or damaged chain. | **Stop immediately.** Adjust tension or replace chain. |
