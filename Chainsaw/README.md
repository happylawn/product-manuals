1. Safety First

WARNING: Read all safety warnings and instructions before use.

Personal Safety

Always wear a safety helmet, goggles, and ear protection.
+2

Use firm leather safety gloves and protective clothing (cut-resistant trousers/dungarees).

Wear safety boots with non-slip soles and steel toes.

Keep all parts of your body away from the saw chain when it is operating.

Work Area Safety

Keep the work area clean and well-lit.

Keep children and bystanders away while operating the tool.

Machine & Battery Safety

Always hold the chain saw with both hands (right hand on rear handle, left hand on front handle).
+2

Beware of kickback and avoid contact with the guide bar tip.
+1

Remove the battery before performing any adjustments or maintenance.
+1

2. Before You Operate
Installing/Removing the Battery


To Install: Slide the battery cartridge in until the red indicator is no longer visible and it clicks into place.


To Remove: Press the button on the front of the cartridge while sliding it out.

Main Power and Mode Control

Turn On: Press the main power button. The mode lamps will light up in green.

Change Speed: Press the Mode button to cycle through the three speed ranges:


High: For trimming, felling, and cutting logs.


Medium: For steady felling and long cutting tasks.


Low (ECO): For cutting small branches and extending run time.


Turn Off: Press the main power button again.

3. Operation Guide
Starting the Saw

Push the lock lever down/forward.

Squeeze the lock-off lever with your palm.

Pull the switch trigger to start.

Release the trigger to stop.

Using the Chain Brake


To Activate: Push the front hand guard forward with the back of your hand. The chain should stop immediately.
+1


To Release: Pull the front hand guard back toward you.

Adjusting Chain Lubrication

You can adjust the oil feed rate by turning the oil adjusting screw on the bottom of the tool using a box wrench.

4. Maintenance

WARNING: Always ensure the tool is off and the battery is removed before maintenance.

Adjusting Saw Chain Tension

Loosen the retaining nuts slightly to loosen the sprocket cover.

Lift the tip of the guide bar.

Turn the chain adjusting screw clockwise to tighten or counterclockwise to loosen.

Tighten the retaining nuts once the chain fits snugly against the lower side of the bar.
+1

Cleaning the Tool

Clean the guide bar groove and oil holes of chips and sawdust regularly.
+2

Wipe the tool body with a dry cloth and keep air vents clear.
+1

5. Troubleshooting

Abnormal vibration.	Cutting tool (chain/bar) is broken, bent, or worn.	Stop immediately. Check and replace the saw chain or guide bar.
Chain doesn't stop.	
Brake band worn or debris in the brake.
